# CoffeeCo Sales Analysis Report
![Coffee Store Image](coffeeco_header.jpg)

## Executive Summary
CoffeeCo required a detailed analysis to provide the CEO with insights that will guide business strategy. The analysis was conducted to determine customer behavior as well as product purchase performance. The findings show that there is a generally very good rating, with the average customer rating at 4 out of 5. The best-selling product, which generated approximately 1.2M INR, is the Cold Brew Coffee Pack, with a significantly higher sales contribution than the second-placed Coffee Beans. The analysis also found that Aditi Gupta is the store's top customer, both in terms of items purchased and total sales amount.

## Business Problem
CoffeeCo is a coffee store that operates in several cities in India. The requirement is data-driven insights. They wanted quality insights to support their strategy as they look to grow the business across the different store locations in which they currently operate. Their key focus areas are customer behavior, product performance, and sales across the cities where they are currently located.

## Objectives
These are the following objectives provided by the business;
	- What is the total sales generated and the average rating on the products based on customer feedback?
	- What are the top best-selling products and which city pulls in the most revenue?
	- Who are the customers with the most purchases and total sales spent?
  
## Data Overview
The analysis was conducted on CoffeeCo’s entire database system. Four datasets were provided: sales, customer, city, and product. The customer, city, and product datasets are the dimension tables, and they store distinct values used in the sales fact table. The sales table is the fact table storing sales transactions from Jan 1, 2023 to Oct 1, 2024. The sales table contained 10,388 sales transaction records.
The customer and product tables are both connected to the sales table with a one-to-many relationship, with the many side of the cardinality on the sales table. The city table is connected to the customer table with a one-to-many relationship, with the many side on the customer table.

## Analysis and Insights
### Sales Performance Overview
  - The total sales generated by the business between Jan 1, 2023 and Oct 1, 2024 is 6,070,190 INR.
  - The average rating for the business is approximately 4 out of 5. This is a very good rating for the business.
    
### Customer & City Analysis
  - The top 3 cities with the highest number of customers are Jaipur with 69 customers, Delhi with 68 customers, and Pune with 52 total customers.
    
### Product Performance
  - The top 5 best-selling products for CoffeeCo are as follows; Cold Brew Coffee Pack (6 Bottles) with a total of approximately 1.2M INR within the year under review. The remaining 4 products in descending order are Coffee Beans with total sales 400k INR less than the top-performing product, Coffee Gift Hamper, Ground Espresso Coffee (250g), and Tote Bag with Coffee Design.
  - There were no customer ratings less than 3 for any transaction or product. This further buttresses the high customer satisfaction mentioned earlier.
    
### Customer Segmentation & Revenue
  - There are only 7 cities with a total sales amount of at least 200K INR. The city with the highest sales amount, topping the second-placed city by approximately 200k INR, is Pune with a total sales amount of about 1.3M INR.
  - A total of seventeen (17) customers made at least 50 purchases from the business within the time frame under review. However, the top customer is Customer with ID 3 - Aditi Gupta, who resides in Bangalore and has made a total of 87 purchases.
    
### Advanced Insights
  - The customer who has spent the most money is Aditi Gupta. This is the same customer from Bangalore with the highest number of purchases. Her total spend is exactly 49,930 INR.

## Recommendations
  - Customer satisfaction has been very good, with an average rating of 4. This indicates great customer service, and it is recommended that this rating be maintained, as it is a core contributing factor to the sales figures.
  - The margin between the top-selling product and the least-selling product is significantly high. The top-selling product generates about 1.2M INR, while the least-selling product generates a mere 16.6K INR. This should be examined to determine the reason for the high margin. Factors such as price, location, and product placement should be considered.
  - Pune is the city with the highest sales amount generated, pulling about 1.3M INR. The next four cities also have figures just a few hundred thousand INR away from reaching a million in sales. In order to close that gap, steps should be taken to replicate the factors contributing to the high sales figures in Pune in the other cities with equal potential. These cities are; Chennai, Bangalore, Jaipur, and Delhi.
